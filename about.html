About

How would I describe myself?

I would most likely describe myself as someone incredibly proactive, organized but also slightly stuborn. However I do believe those are the atributes that allow me to persevere through my work until I'm completely satisfied with it every time.


What drew me into being an artist?

Ever since I was a child, I've always been surrounded by art due to my mom's influence: she has always enjoyed painting, embroidering, and experimenting with several materials and techniques, while also doing commisions and having a full time job.

However it was hard for me to actually decide to persue art due to the bad stigma that artists have in the work industry. I am just incredibly proud of myself for actually going after what I wanted.


Where am I from and how does that affect my work?

I was born and raised in Portugal but I don’t believe that had much of an influence regarding my creations, specially since I seek inspiration from both national and international artists.


How has my education helped in developing my art?

My education has been a huge influence in my improvement as an artist, ever since 2019 (when I was first introduced to the arts high school IAI) I’ve come a long way and got to really experiment with different mediums and subjects, gaining a new point of view over matters that seemed otherwise irrelevant to the art world. Besides that, it also allowed me to make friends who share the same passion as I do.


What projects did I most enjoy being a part of?

In 11th grade I got to focus a lot more in projects revolving around video (specially Short Films), which were my favorite projects to work on, especially participating in Bgreen, a contest whose goal was to make a 45 second film regarding that year’s theme, “ Earthability”.


Favorite mediums/ types of art?

Out of all the materials and techniques I’ve experimented with, my favorite has to be either grafite or oil pastels. The first one takes me a lot more time to work with but never fails to give me an incredibly realistic result that always fascinates me; the second allows me to be a lot more creative and so it’s more entertaining to use. However I’m always open to experiment with new things.


What motivates me to create?

Being able to go through my old art work and immediately notice my progress and how much I’ve improved is for sure a big part of my source of motivation. Besides the way my friends always hype me up while I draw/ work makes me want to do more and succeed for them and also for me, so that I’m able to look back to this moment and know for sure that I did the best I could at all times.
